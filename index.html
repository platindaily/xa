The Birth of the NFL

On September 17, 1920, representatives from 10 professional football teams met in Canton, Ohio, and formed the American Professional Football Association (APFA), which later became the National Football League. What started as a small gathering has since become one of the most powerful sports organizations in the world.

The Early Years (1920s‚Äì1930s)

The league struggled for stability but gradually gained credibility. Teams like the Chicago Bears and Green Bay Packers became foundational franchises that still thrive today.

Post-War Expansion (1940s‚Äì1960s)

Following World War II, football became a national obsession. The NFL expanded to new cities, integrated players of color, and merged with the rival AFL in 1970.
